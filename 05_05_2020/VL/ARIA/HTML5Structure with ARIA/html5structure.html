Landmark Navigation Examples

Using a Screen Reader to Navigate Landmarks

  • JAWS: Use the semicolon ( ; ) key to go forward through landmarks, or shift + semicolon to go backward.
  • NVDA: Use the "d" key to go forward through landmarks, or shift + d to go backward
  • VoiceOver: Control + Option + right arrow (until "Landmarks" shows), then use the down or up arrows to navigate the list.

HTML 5 and ARIA and aria-labels

This is a header with role="banner" and aria-label="header".

  • JAWS says "header banner"
  • NVDA says "banner landmark"
  • VoiceOver says "Header with 2 items. Banner"

This is a nav with role="navigation" and aria-label="main navigation".

  • JAWS says "main navigation navigation region"
  • NVDA says "navigation landmark"
  • VoiceOver says "main navigation navigation"

This is a main with role="main" and aria-label="main content".

  • JAWS says "main content main region" (JAWS pronounces it as "main con-TENT main region")
  • NVDA says "main landmark"
  • VoiceOver says "Main content with 6 items. Main" (The 6 items are the paragraph, the list, then the 4 divs.)

This is a section with role="region" and aria-label="section one".

  • JAWS says "section one region"
  • NVDA does not treat region as a landmark
  • VoiceOver does not treat region as a landmark

This is a section with role="region" and aria-label="section two".

  • VoiceOver does not treat region as a landmark

This is an article with role="article" and aria-label="The sky is falling!"

  • VoiceOver does not treat article as a landmark

This is an aside with role="complimentary" and aria-label="sidebar".

  • JAWS says "sidebar complimentary information"

  • VoiceOver says "Sidebar with 2 items. Complementary" (The 2 items are the paragraph and the list.)

This is a footer with role="contentinfo" and aria-label="footer".

  • JAWS says "footer content information" (JAWS pronounces it as "footer con-TENT information")
